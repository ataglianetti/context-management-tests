# Persona: Startup CEO

## Phase 1: About You

**Job title and industry:** CEO and co-founder of Canopy, an edtech startup. We build AI-powered tutoring tools for K-12 school districts. Series A, 28 employees.

**Expertise:** Product strategy, fundraising, school district sales cycles, K-12 education policy, team building at early stage. Technical enough to read code and understand architecture decisions but not an engineer — my co-founder/CTO handles that. Former teacher (5 years), then edtech PM at Pearson, then started Canopy.

**Good day at work:** Closing a district deal, seeing a teacher demo go well, or having a board meeting where the metrics speak for themselves. Basically: when the product is clearly working for kids and the business reflects it.

**Communication preferences:** I think out loud. I'll give you a stream of consciousness and I need you to help me organize it, push back on the weak parts, and sharpen the strong parts. Don't just validate — I get enough of that from my team. Be concise in writing but thorough in analysis when I ask for it.

## Phase 2: Your Organizations

**Primary context:** Canopy — AI tutoring platform for K-12. 28 people: 12 engineering, 6 sales/CS, 4 product/design, 3 content (former teachers who build curriculum), 3 ops/finance. Series A ($8M) closed 8 months ago, ~18 months runway at current burn.

**Board:**
- Lead investor is Megan Torres at Owl Ventures (board seat). Supportive but metrics-focused. Wants to see net revenue retention and district expansion numbers every board meeting.
- Second board seat is my co-founder/CTO, Raj Patel. We're aligned on product vision but disagree on hiring pace — he wants to slow down, I want to accelerate sales hiring.
- Board observer: David Kim, angel investor and former superintendent. Invaluable for district relationships and credibility.

**Key dynamics:**
- School district sales cycles are 6-18 months. We have 12 paying districts and a pipeline of 40+. The challenge is converting pipeline to revenue before runway gets tight.
- Product-market fit is strong in math tutoring (grades 3-8). We're debating expansion into ELA (English Language Arts) — big market but dilutes engineering focus.
- My co-founder relationship is the most important thing in the company. We disagree productively but I need to make sure it stays that way as pressure increases.
- Hiring is critical. Need a VP Sales yesterday. Two final candidates, very different profiles.

**Secondary context:** I sit on the advisory board of TeachFirst (education nonprofit). Light commitment — 2 hours/month. Mostly gives me access to district leaders.

## Phase 5: Thinking Partner

**Hardest part of decisions:** Everything is consequential and partially irreversible at this stage. Hiring the wrong VP Sales costs 6 months. Expanding into ELA too early could kill us. Not expanding could let a competitor lock up the market. I need someone to help me think through second-order effects because I tend to move fast and figure it out later.

**Useful pushback:** Challenge my optimism. I'm a natural seller — I can convince myself that anything will work. Push me on assumptions, especially around timeline and resource requirements. Also: help me see when I'm neglecting the co-founder relationship for the sake of speed.

**Keeps me up at night:** Running out of runway before we hit Series B metrics. Specifically: if the VP Sales hire doesn't work out, we don't hit $3M ARR, and we're raising from a position of weakness. The ELA decision is secondary but connected — it's a bet on a bigger TAM that could either accelerate the raise or scatter our focus.
