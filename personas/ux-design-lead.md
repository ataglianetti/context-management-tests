# Persona: UX Design Lead

## Phase 1: About You

**Job title and industry:** Senior UX Designer / Design Lead at Vantage, a B2B SaaS company that makes project management software. ~300 employees.

**Expertise:** UX design, design systems, user research, prototyping, information architecture, accessibility (WCAG AA), design ops. 8 years in product design — started as a visual designer, moved into UX, now lead design for the Workflows product area. Figma power user. I can read code (HTML/CSS, enough React to understand component props) but I don't write it in production.

**Good day at work:** When research reveals something we didn't expect and it actually changes the direction of a feature. When an engineer messages me that the component I designed just shipped and it looks exactly right. When I have a 2-hour focus block with no meetings and actually use it.

**Communication preferences:** Visual thinker. I process better with structure — tables, diagrams, side-by-side comparisons. Don't give me a wall of text when a table would do. I'm direct but not blunt — I work with a lot of engineers and PMs who have strong opinions, and I've learned to frame critique constructively without being passive. Skip the design jargon definitions — I know what a heuristic evaluation is.

## Phase 2: Your Organizations

**Primary context:** Vantage — B2B project management SaaS. ~300 employees, Series D. My team is 4 product designers + 1 UX researcher + 1 content designer. I'm IC lead for the Workflows product area (not a people manager — our VP Design manages the team, I lead design direction).

**Manager:** VP of Design, Rebecca Huang. She's building out the design org — hired me specifically to establish design system rigor. Advocates hard for the team but sometimes overcommits us. She reports to the CPO, Jason Park, who's product-first and sees design as a service function — I'm trying to shift that perception.

**Key dynamics:**
- Tension between sprint velocity and design quality. Engineering wants specs by Wednesday, design needs research time. I'm always negotiating for an extra day.
- Design system v2 is my big initiative — migrating from a Figma-only library to a coded component system (Storybook + tokens). Engineering buy-in is partial.
- Research debt is real. We ship features based on PM intuition more than user evidence. Our researcher, Aisha, is at 150% capacity.
- Content design is undervalued. Jordan (content designer) catches UX writing issues that would confuse users, but their feedback often comes too late in the process because they're looped in at the end.

**Secondary context:** Freelance design consulting — I take 1-2 small projects per quarter. Currently have one active client (a fintech startup, NovaPay, doing a mobile app redesign). ~5-8 hours/week. Keeps my skills sharp on different problem spaces and supplements income.

## Phase 5: Thinking Partner

**Hardest part of decisions:** Knowing when "good enough" is actually good enough. I can polish a design forever, but the real skill is knowing when the last 10% of refinement isn't worth the sprint delay. Also: building the case for design quality in a product-org that measures velocity, not craft.

**Useful pushback:** Challenge me when I'm over-designing. Tell me when a simpler solution would ship faster and still solve the user problem. Also push me on stakeholder framing — I tend to lead with design rationale when I should lead with business impact. Help me translate "better UX" into "lower churn" or "higher activation."

**Keeps me up at night:** The design system stalling. If engineering doesn't adopt it, we've built an expensive Figma library that doesn't actually improve consistency. And research debt — we're making big bets on features without enough user evidence, and one of them is going to bite us.

## Phase 6: Workflow

**Typical week:** Monday is planning — review Figma comments, check Linear board, prioritize design work for the week. Tuesday-Wednesday is heads-down design work (Figma, prototyping, design system component work). Thursday is feedback day — design critiques, PM reviews, eng handoff sessions. Friday is research and freelance — Dovetail synthesis, NovaPay work.

**Tools:** Figma (primary design), FigJam (workshops/brainstorms), Dovetail (research), Slack, Linear for project tracking, Loom for async design walkthroughs, Notion (team docs).

**Meetings:** 8-10 per week. 1:1 with Rebecca, weekly design crit (when it happens), Workflows sprint planning, Workflows retro, PM sync with Liam, eng handoff with Maya, monthly design review with Jason, ad-hoc research debriefs.

**Code repos:** None that I own. I reference the design system repo (`vantage/design-system`) and sometimes the main app repo (`vantage/workflows-app`) to check component implementation.
